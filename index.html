The trick

Empowering with ownership
  • Growing
  • Scaling
“ If you wanna go fast go alone if you wanna go far go together ”
  • So WHY ???
  • Surely many of us have worked with a manager (not leader) who was all about "do this, in this way, by this time and then repeat 1000 times"
  • The kind of manager that's just a bottleneck and strives to be irreplaceable, fueled by ego and insecurities
  • The truth is that nobody is irreplaceable, so why don't just embrace the fact that your are repleceable and try to be exependable possibly to be replaced by somebody that have grown and learned with you?
  • At the same time many want to grow and learn, want to get better at facing today's challenges and want to be ready to face more challenging tomorrows
  • Well then the only way is trying to be expendable empowering others and give them ownership
  • It is a way of building legacy and make room for others so that at some point in time the current leader can step aside, to keep growing in the same way his/her team can

Mission and Vision

Narrative
  1. Explicit objectives
  2. Firm vision
  3. Clear communication
  4. Constant feedback
  5. Support in difficult times
  • Narrative is ever evolving and must be kept alive
  • Explicit objectives (WHAT): deliverying a given product or functionality, reaching a given outcome of a given plan, participate on a given process, but you have to narrow it down
  • Firm Vision (FOR WHAT REASON): to learn a given thing, to scale up to a certain degree, to bring a value internally or to a customer, basically to grow
  • Clear communication (HOW): to avoid misunderstandings, to align the group to the cause and its outcomes
  • Constant feedback (HOW): fail fast recover quickly, to learn, to keep the alignemnt to the cause
  • Support in difficult times (HOW): fire and forget will not work as per the above, to not let the team struggle alone
  • I am going to share an experience with you that is about team leading but is narrowed down to the implementation of a given funcionality in the team, my point is that leading is not something that you can do on every scale, from being the CEO to being en engineer facing a given tech challenge
  • --- Disco: the wip functionality was reviewed and evaluated periodically and the team was aware of the wins and the fails
  • --- Disco: content distribution system needed to have a content staging functionality to let content editors approve content prior to releasing it to customers
  • --- Disco: to bring value to the client and the customer, to offload work to non-tech folks, to learn
  • --- Disco: the plan was created and agreed upon by every team member, the responsibilities were set
  • --- Disco: the team needed to be unblocked or advised from time to time, needed to be taken care of given the difficulity of the task
  • Let's stop for a moment and let's ask ourselves if these are things that exclusively the team led should take care of
  • --- The answer if clearly no, if you manage to have every team member interiorizing the goals and the narrative around them you'll have everything you need

Mission and Vision

Common language
  • Team agreements
  • Playbook
  • Docs
  • Overcommunication
  • how can you be sure that the team is aligned on the mission and vision? by being sure that everybody is speaking the same language
  • Team agreements: it is not the lead deciding how to do things but the team, they commit to something they defined themselves, TAs are powerful because the define the rules to be part of the tribu
  • --- Ecommerce: naming conventions, PR templates, commits messages, definition of done, no major prod releases on fridays -> Nuria shared some examples
  • Playbook: set of rules / suggestions / strategies to follow when pursuing the mission, applies to teams whose mission cannot be easily narrowed down because of the variety of situations they have to face
  • --- Architecture: Presales / Projects support / Tech excellence -> Templates, processes, handy material / weekly sync invites, prt, mentoring lines / chapters
  • Docs: the most lean, updated and autogenerated form of documentation possible
  • --- Ecommerce: Sync and Async docs, architecture diagrams, readMe
  • Overcommunication: you end up creating a vocabulary that fits the mission
  • --- Ecommerce: the magic happens -> non tech folks understanding tech stuff -> backoffice endpoints
  • --- DISCO: the magic happens -> non tech folks understanding tech stuff -> fixtures as docs + dry run endpoints

Mission and Vision

Facts
  • Metrics
  • Reports
  • Dashboards
  • Notes
  • Leading a team to achieve a end goal is like fliying a plane you need instruments and a cockpit
  • The more you can get the subjectiveness out of it the better so that the team as a whole is led by facts
  • Facts will allow you to work on actinable items
  • Metrics --- presales (actors, type / size of engagement, lost / won reasons)
  • Metrics --- mood tool
  • Reports --- prt (tech agnostic checks, testing, security, monitoring, logging, etc.)
  • Reports --- delivery status report (client satisfaction, team happiness, budget, etc)
  • Dashboards --- performance (infrastructure), performance (services), business logic (number of orders in ecommerce)
  • Notes --- One on one shared notes, SBI shared doc

Teammates


  • So HOW ???
  • Know your team and let your teammates know eachothers

Teammates

Physical system
  1. Every person is a whole word
  2. Every team is a system
  3. Every system obeys physical laws
  • Start from the assumption that you have almost no context about your teammates, their history, their drivers, theirs fears, nothing
  • Work as much as possible to unveil a percentage of themselves, who they really are, and create an enviroment in which they feel secure to share that
  • You are not dealing with software engineers, delivery managers, UX/UI designers, but with people
  • Only then the team will start to understand which are the pieces of the system
  • And only then you can start to understand how the system behaves and reacts to internal and external forces
  • Which are the inputs that make it rigid or elastic
  • So that in the end you are able to understand how and when to apply pressure in order to increase the elasticity and not the rigidness
  • E.g. Ecommerce: I was TL then I went off then back again as senior dev in the project
  • E.g. Ecommerce: I knew the system and the pieces in it because I was there
  • E.g. Ecommerce: There were 5 main backend services and huge frontend
  • E.g. Ecommerce: the team was tired and unfocused
  • E.g. Ecommerce: I did not want to apply more pressure to reach the deadline directly on the team
  • E.g. Ecommerce: I decided to apply pressure on the TL given the fact that I knew how capable he was, he needed just some pressure and a bit of focus
  • E.g. Ecommerce: I did not modify any equilibrium publicly
  • E.g. Ecommerce: I divided the tasks and the backend services were 1 per person whereas the frontend was on the TL
  • E.g. Ecommerce: I know for a fact that the teammates understood themselves as a physical system and better understood how to work together

Teammates

Profiling
  1. Performers
  2. Doers
  3. Less effective doers
  4. Potential trouble sources
  5. Suppressive persons
  • Now if we forget for a moment that every person is a whole world and we work under the assumption that we have unveiled as much as possible about them
  • Performers: always pushing with strong sense of responsibility
  • Doers: right attitude to improve, and learn
  • Less effective doers: their willingness to help ends at the edges of their comfort zone
  • Potential trouble source: unpredictables because of up and downs sometimes they helps other times they destroy
  • Suppressive persons: actively attempts to destroy behind a mask of trying to help
  • We usually work whit the first 3 types and all of them are valid and create a good balance in every organization
  • When it comes the team the only way to create a safe space is by using non negotiable values, usually companies have them
  • Values are like team agreements on steroid and in the same way as the team agreements are tool that can be used by anybody
  • I undirectly and directly have seen the damage done by the last two types of teammates
  • Just give your team the means to deal with them and stood up for themselves because that's the higher form of empowerment

Tasks

Balance
  • Leveraging strengths
  • Improving weaknesses
  • Challenging
  • Achievable
  • Again it is all about ownership and empowering others
  • You want to make them face challenges that are slightly over their capabilities
  • Leveraging strengths & Improving weaknesses --- you cannot ask a fish to climb a tree
  • Challenging & Achievable --- you cannot throw impossible stuff at your team
  • Most of the times you'll have to walk the walk by divide and conquer
  • You get one big, challenging yet achievable task and you'll have to sit together to
  • --- planning, documenting, risk assessing and follow up its evolution
  • --- and most important you do not have to expect that the HOW is your how but the WHAT is the proper what
  • --- e.g. I should have followed the ppt template that dorado provided me for this KS but I decided not to and Jose is going to be happy about the WHAT and not the HOW
  • E.g. Disco and storage choice, CosmosDB had bad performances, we had to really be sure about it and find a proper alternative (Azure Storages)
  • You'll end up doing a lot of unchallenging and shitty tasks just to let the team being focused on the things that really matter

Tasks

Safety nets
  1. Setting up
  2. Shadowing
  3. Reviewing
  4. Backing up
  • Set up --- is a must, the enviroment your team works in must be as failure proof as possible
  • E.g. 3 envs (dev, stg, pro) - testing (unit, integration, e2e) - CI/CD with rollback
  • Shadowing --- it may be needed, mostly the first times a given team member is facing a new challege
  • E.g. Ecommerce the 4 backend microservices, I did one and shadowed the teammates when doing the others
  • Reviewing --- it may be needed, mostly the teammate have already faced the new challegne but stil does not master it
  • E.g. presales activities, coming up with and presenting a proposal to the client
  • Backing up --- it is always needed when the first 3 things were not needed or possible
  • E.g. I was on paternity leave and I just said "guys I wont be there, I completely trust you and your judgment and will back you up always no matter how bad you think you messed it up during my absence"
  • Never fire and forget

Tasks

Mistakes
  • Dynamics
  • Postmortems
  • No blaming
  • There should be transparency and openess about errors, embrace them and celebrate them is a must
  • E.g. infinitas HE post mortem was shared with the whole company by a teammate that embraced that culture
  • --- there was a huge amount of details in there and no shame in sharing them
  • We aim to --- Fail quick and recover fast
  • Empowering the team to activate the "fire mode"

The lead


  • So HOW ???
  • We already understood that is not about the lead but still let's mention it

The lead

Servant
  • Zero ego
  • Open & transparent
  • Rules bending
  • As we already stated, if you are there for the label and the power, well you'll become a bottleneck and the team cannot scale or grow
  • The best leads I have crossed my path with were humble, and had no ego at all
  • Actually the best one were the ones that did not wanted to end up being a lead ---
  • --- they were not actively asking for a promotion but on the contrary just hempling everybody, walking the walk, supporting, celebrating, unblocking
  • As before mentioned you'll end up doing a lot of shitty tasks as a lead but that's fine, because you jop is to clear the path for the others
  • And in the end you'll have a team that would walk on path to complete a mission that is not horizontal, but it would, on average, have a grwoing trend
  • and here come the importance of the transparency and openess, in order to make that path a growing one you'll have to deal with the context just above and just below it, giving to the team only the center one
  • every time you communicate you should ask yourself "what for am I saying this" not "why" but "what for"
  • and at the same time given the fact that you are creating this growing path you'll need some rule bending in it since bascially you are walking a piece of new path every time

The lead

Passionate
  • It's contagious
  • Celebrate the wins
  • Team's problems are yours
  • Every time you navigate through some hard times passion is what get you out of it
  • Its like the purest form of motivation and man I believe it is safe to say that we have experienced some hard times in this company
  • What keeps the passion alive? celebrating the wins, it does no matter how small they look like they are still one step toward a better future
  • And never forget to give the team credit for the wins and to bring home with yourself the losses
  • And finally remember that team's problems are yours and aiming for expendability means that teams culture should be about the problem of others are my problems as well
